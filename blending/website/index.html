Part 0: Unsharpen
For this part, we applied the Unsharpen Mask Filter onto images in order to "sharpen" them. This was done by taking a low pass of the original image, subtracting it from the original image, and then adding the leftover high frequencies to the original image (a portion of those values we call the alpha value) in order to accentuate the edges of the image, causing the image to appear sharper.

Part 1: Hybrid
For this part of the project we took two images that were similar in some manner - shape, size, color, etc - and created a hybrid image of the two by taking the low frequencies from one of them, and the higher frequencies of the other, and overlaying them on top of each other. What this provided is the low frequencies being more visible from a closer distance, and the higher frequencies appearing as more prominent from a farther distance. Much like the famous Marilyn Monroe and Albert Einstein image, this "tricks" our eyes into to seeing two images in one - just at different distances. I successfully applied this to a picture of a Pepperoni Pizza and out lovely Moon that encircles our planet.
